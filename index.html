Types of Vermouth

A brief guide to one of the most iconic aperitifs in the world.

What is Vermouth?

Vermouth is an aromatized fortified wine flavored with various botanicals such as herbs, spices, and roots. Originally created for medicinal purposes, it has become a key ingredient in many classic cocktails and an elegant drink on its own.

Main Types of Vermouth

Red Vermouth (Rosso)

Known for its sweet, rich and slightly bitter flavor. Typically Italian and used in cocktails like Negroni or Manhattan.

White Vermouth (Bianco)

Smoother and lighter than red vermouth, with a delicate sweetness. It’s perfect to serve on ice with a slice of lemon.

Dry Vermouth

Usually French in origin, this type is more herbal and less sweet. It’s essential in cocktails like the classic Martini.

Rosé Vermouth

A modern and aromatic style with fruity and floral notes. Excellent as a refreshing summer aperitif.

How to Enjoy Vermouth

Serve it chilled, with ice, citrus peel, or soda. Pair it with olives, cheese, or light tapas for the perfect aperitivo experience.
